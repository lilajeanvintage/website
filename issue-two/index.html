Issue Two

What's in Issue Two?

Nostalgia

How could a commemorative war issue not contain bucket loads of nostalgia! We present an exclusive interview with Evelyn Hill, one of the few remaining war brides of WWII, we look at life at a munitions factory during WWII through the diary entries of Bettie May Hayward, as well as second hand stories passed down through photographs and objects.

Fashion

Shot on location at a stunning period home, we look to the iconic 1940s and the women who spent many days and nights alone during WWII. Many of the pieces used in the shoot were sourced from charity shops to demonstrate that you don't always have to break the bank to achieve a vintage inspired look.

Grooming

Milena Kesic from The Leopard Lounge show us how to achieve some 1940s victory rolls.

Features

Elizabeth Little explores the upcoming exhibition Your Friend the Enemy at SH Ervin Gallery, Ellen Driscoll presents the crafts used to both distract from and support WWI and we look into the history and significance of the ANZAC War Memorial in Sydney's Hyde Park and the Shrine of Remembrance in Melbourne. There is also a feature on the significant role of women during the First World War, which is so often overlooked, as well as some powerful images of women during WWI and II.

Interviews

We talk to Melbourne vintage blogger and one time shop owner Trish Hunter about her new venture The Vintage Post, as well as Katie-Louise Ford and Tim Nicol, the dynamic duo behind the new fashion label The Gold Hatted Lovers.
