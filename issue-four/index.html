Issue Four

What's in Issue Four | Spring 2015

Nostalgia

Travel forms such a large part of our culture but people didn't always venture abroad as often as they do now. We asked people to reflect on their travels and these are their stories.

Fashion

Taking inspiration from the 1950s, we reflect on the glamour that once surrounded travel. It was often more about the journey than the destination so it was important to look good! Gorgeous dresses supplied by The Vintage Drawer in Sydney.

Grooming

Our resident beauty expert Margeaux Vallantyne aka The Distinctive Dame, shows us how to achieve a simple 1950s style up-do - perfect for travelling during the warmer months.

Features

When it opened at Melbourne's Capitol Theatre in 1924, Cecil B. DeMille's The Ten Commandments ran for nineteen weeks. The local Picture Palace was a thing of beauty, many crafted in the popular Art Deco style, and containing only one screen. We reflect on the cinemas that are still standing and those that have long been a memory. We look at Ball Season in Sydney, when anyone who was anyoe was at the Black & White Ball at the Trocadero, and we look back to the father of modern make-up - Max Factor.

Interviews

We speak with International Burlesque star Dita Von Teese about her recent trip to Australia and her collaboration with Wheels & Dollbaby. Vintage aficionado Doris Raymond gives us her tips on buying vintage and discusses her L.A store The Way We Wore.
