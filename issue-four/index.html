Issue Four

What's in Issue Four | Spring 2015

Nostalgia

We had some amazing reader submissions while putting this issue together and highlight some wonderfully glamorous memories in our third issue. As usual we present our regular section - I remember when and This old thing - as we encourage people to delve into their old photos.

Fashion

Taking inspiration from the early 1960s, we follow the rise of a young starlett from on set angst to Oscar glory. With gorgeous clothing from The Vintage Drawer and Potts Point Vintage as well as some over the top jewels from Trésors, we recreate stunning black and white publicity stills from the Golden Age of Hollywood.

Grooming

We introduce you to Margeaux Vallantyne aka The Distinctive Dame, our new grooming expert. She shows us how to achieve the perfect Bridgette Bardot beeehive with an easy to follow step-by-step guide.

Features

When it opened at Melbourne's Capitol Theatre in 1924, Cecil B. DeMille's The Ten Commandments ran for nineteen weeks. The local Picture Palace was a thing of beauty, many crafted in the popular Art Deco style, and containing only one screen. We reflect on the cinemas that are still standing and those that have long been a memory. We look at Ball Season in Sydney, when anyone who was anyoe was at the Black & White Ball at the Trocadero, and we look back to the father of modern make-up - Max Factor.

Interviews

With Australian costume drama making a big comeback this year with Love Child and A Place to Call Home returning to our screens, we speak with costume designer Xanthe Heubel about her work on both series and about what inspires her. We chat to Perth blogger Ally Moschini of The Vintage Valley and explore the treasure trove that is Potts Point Vintage.
